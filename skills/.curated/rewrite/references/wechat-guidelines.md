# WeChat Official Account Publishing Guidelines

These are the key principles followed when rewriting content for WeChat Official Accounts.

## Readability on Mobile

1. **Short paragraphs**: 1-3 sentences maximum per paragraph. WeChat is read mostly on mobile, long paragraphs are overwhelming.

2. **Generous spacing**: Always leave a blank line between paragraphs. This improves scrolling comfort.

3. **Subheadings frequently**: Add a subheading every 300-500 words to break up text and help scanning.

4. **Bullet points**: Convert long lists to bullet points for easy scanning.

## Tone Guidelines

Most WeChat Official Accounts benefit from a conversational, approachable tone:

- Address the reader directly using "你" (you) when appropriate
- Avoid overly academic or bureaucratic language
- Start with a hook - draw the reader in the first paragraph
- End with a conclusion that summarizes key points or encourages engagement
- Keep sentences relatively short

## Formatting Best Practices

- Use `**bold**` to emphasize key points and important terms
- Avoid italic text - it doesn't display well on all WeChat clients
- Use horizontal rules (`---`) to separate major sections
- Avoid complex tables - they render poorly on mobile WeChat
- Keep images between paragraphs, not in the middle of paragraphs
- Images should be relevant and add value, not just decoration

## Content Structure

Good structure for WeChat articles:

1. **Opening hook** (1 short paragraph) - draw the reader in
2. **Main content** - split into 3-6 main sections with clear headings
3. **Images** - inserted after every 2-3 short sections or every 300-500 words
4. **Conclusion** - summarize key takeaways
5. **Call to action** (optional) - encourage reading, sharing, or comments
6. **Original source attribution** (if repurposing content)

## Image Placement

- Place images at natural breaking points between sections
- Avoid breaking up a single paragraph with an image
- One image per section is usually enough
- Ensure images are relevant to the adjacent text
- Include captions when helpful for context
- For AI-generated images, use high-quality prompts that match the article's theme and tone
- WeChat works best with images at 16:9 or 4:3 aspect ratio for header images

## What to Avoid

- Walls of text - these get skipped immediately on mobile
- Long, complex sentences that are hard to follow
- Heavy use of slang if targeting a general audience
- Jargon without explanation when writing for general audience
- Too many images that distract from the content
- Large images that slow down loading (we let WeChat handle this)

## Metadata Tips for WeChat

- Keep titles under 64 characters (WeChat display limit)
- Use emojis in titles sparingly - one is enough if used
- Make the description engaging to encourage clicks in feeds
- Add appropriate tags/categories if your WeChat account supports it
